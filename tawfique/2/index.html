A paragraph is a group of related sentences that support one central idea. In general, paragraphs consist of three parts: A topic sentence which contains the controlling idea, body sentences which provide details, context, and/or examples supporting the topic sentence, and the concluding or the bridge sentence to the next paragraph or section of the paper. Paragraphs show where the subdivisions of a research paper begin and end and, thus, help the reader see the organization of the essay and grasp its main points in relation to the research problem.
